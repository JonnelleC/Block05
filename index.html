Zoo Animals

Polar Bears

Polar bears, the majestic lords of the Arctic, are one of the region’s most popular and charismatic species. These powerful predators, known for their thick fur and strong swimming abilities, primarily inhabit the sea ice of the Arctic Ocean

Giraffes

Both the legs and neck of the animal are extremely elongate. The body is short-coupled and the back is sloped upwards from the tail to the base of the neck. There are two ossicones, known by most as “horns” on the skull and a large bump in the middle of the forehead. The eyelashes are quite long and thick.

Lions

Except for a small population of the Indian lion subspecies that remains in the Gir Forest of northwest India, lions now live only in Africa, from the Sahara's southern fringe to northern South Africa. They are absent from equatorial areas dominated by moist tropical forest.

Monkeys

Monkeys originated in Africa and the first group known to have reached South America are thought to have migrated there up to 40 million years ago, when the land masses were probably between 1500 and 2000 kilometres apart, around a quarter of the distance now.

Alligators

Alligators are native to the United States and China. The American alligator (Alligator mississippiensis) is found in the southeastern United States and a small part of northeastern Mexico. The Chinese alligator (A. sinensis) is native to the Yangtze River region of China.
